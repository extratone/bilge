Rediscovering Gran Turismo

After 10 years, my favorite racing simulator has only gotten better.

On December 22nd of last year, I made a big mistake: I drove drunk, got into an accident, and had my driver’s license taken away. I was also driving without insurance, so the (quite extensive) damage done to my little Jetta wagon has been left unrepaired, and it will soon be sold for scrap. These are not decisions I would recommend, but they have led to a sort of positive: I have rediscovered my childhood love of Gran Turismo in the newest title of the series, Gran Turismo Sport. Driving has always been a very important part of my existence — the most important, debatably. I grew up on a farm in rural Missouri, where I began driving by myself around 6–7 and bought my first car — a white 1990 4-door Geo Metro for $100 — at age eleven. Great memories include powerslides on an entirely flooded and frozen 40-acre pasture and max speed runs on the mile-long gravel straight that passed by our two house places to the tune of “Born to be Wild.”

However, during the schoolyear, I lived with my mother in suburbia, where I had to contain my automotive enthusiasm within my Playstation and the original Gran Turismo, which I did not fully understand as a very young man, certainly. It wasn’t until my now-brother-in-law bought me a Playstation 2 and Gran Turismo 3 (happy 19th birthday!) that I really began to care about what I was doing. Perhaps ironically, GT3 would have more of a musical influence on my early childhood than anything else.
