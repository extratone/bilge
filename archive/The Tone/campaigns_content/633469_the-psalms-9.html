MODERNISM
In this month's issue, we'll take a look at Modernism.

what is it?
Modernism is a philosophical movement with roots in the late 19th century. Modernism rejected the modes of thinking that arose during the Enlightenment and embraced the new, whether it was art, architecture, or literature.

who is modernist?
The Modernism label is broad one, and many artists and thinkers can be considered to belong to the movement. The poet Ezra Pound and biologist Charles Darwin, along with artists Pablo Picasso and Jackson Pollock, composer Arnold Schoenberg, and architect Frank Lloyd Wright are some notable examples.

'The modern artist is working with space and time and expressing his feelings rather than illustrating.'
— Jackson Pollock
